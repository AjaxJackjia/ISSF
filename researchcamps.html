ISSF Research Camp

Camp Chairs:

Ravi Bapna (U Minnesota), Bart Baesens (KU Leuven), Sang-Pil Han (ASU)


Objectives of the Research Camp

  • This research camp aims to facilitate the researchers (‘participants’ hereinafter) to improve the quality of their academic papers in the domain of smart finance and related fields through constructive feedback and critical comments from experienced and renowned scholars (‘mentors’ hereinafter)

Benefits to Participants

  • They can receive timely and relevant feedback on their work-in-progress papers from potential reviewers, associate editors, or/and senior editors in top-tier journals in information systems including MISQ and ISR.
  • They can establish personal relationships with mentors and potentially collaborate on their current or/and future projects.

Benefits to Mentors

  • They can find new collaborators who can potentially invite them to mainland China for collaboration or/and have good access to rich empirical data sources.
  • Each participating mentor will be financially awarded by the research camp.
  • Best mentors determined by the helpfulness score they receive from authors will receive cash award.
  • Camp participants and camp chairs will vote the best mentors and award them with prizes.

Matching Mechanism

  • We (camp chairs) seek out potential mentors from our own universities and post the call-for-mentors as well as call-for-participants in the AISWorld Listserv.
  • As we recruit both participants and mentors, we assign a paper to the most relevant mentor through EasyChairs or IdeaWorks system.
  • When a mentor returns his/her review report, we pass it along to the author(s) and ask them how helpful the review is.
